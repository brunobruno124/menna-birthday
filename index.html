March 7th, 2025

My dearest Menna,

Happy birthday, hayati. Your real one — the 7th — the one I've been quietly counting down to for weeks.

I wanted to send you a proper letter, the kind with an envelope and a stamp and your name written slowly on the front. But between the post office, the border, and honestly — auntie Azza — a digital one felt safer. So here we are.

I sit here in Croatia thinking about how far Egypt is, and somehow it still doesn't feel real that the same sky stretches between us. I am proud of you every single day, Manoony — the long hospital shifts, the cases you carry home in your head, the doctor you are becoming. I mean it with everything in me.

This winter, I want to come find you. Walk through your city, sit somewhere warm with you, and just be in the same room for a while. That's all I want.

And one day — not too far from now — we're going to travel somewhere neither of us has been. A lot of somewheres, actually. There will be a little dog too. You'll name her. I've already decided that.

Make a wish, habibty. 🤍

With everything,
Bruno
